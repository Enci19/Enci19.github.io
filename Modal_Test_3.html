Generative Design
A model that leverages artificial intelligence to mimic nature’s evolutionary approach to generate 3D geometries based on a set of parameters and goals input by the designer.

STATUS: COMMERCIALLY AVAILABLE

Given spatial constraints, applied loads, material and manufacturing process as input, the software outputs several (potentially up to hundreds) alternative geometries; these solutions are the evolutionary result of thousands - even millions - testing configurations the software cycles through, learning what works and what doesn’t from each iteration. The resulting bodies satisfy the parameters and reach a predetermined goal set by the designer - the most common ones being maximising stiffness and minimising mass. The results, particularly if developed with more flexible manufacturing processes in mind such as additive manufacturing, are surprisingly organic, as the solutions shares a resemblance with structures that are found in the natural world - a byproduct of the way the generative algorithms, based on genetic algorithms and machine learning, learn and iterate from every generation of simulation.
